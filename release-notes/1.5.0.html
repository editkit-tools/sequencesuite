Release notes

SequenceSuite 1.5.0

A guided welcome for first launches, drag-to-reorder export columns, and proper support for reversed clips in EDLs - plus automatic update checks so you don't have to remember to look.

Welcome and guided tour

  • A new welcome panel greets the first launch - drop a sequence straight onto it or browse for one. Re-open it any time from Help ▸ Welcome to SequenceSuite.
  • A short guided tour points out the mode switcher, sidebar, inspector and export controls after your first sequence opens.
  • The welcome page also shows where your trial or licence stands, with licence entry right there.

Automatic update checks

  • SequenceSuite now checks for updates once a day in the background, and quietly shortly after launch - so a new release reaches you without a trip to the menu bar.
  • Turn it off in Settings ▸ General, where you'll also find Check Now and when the last check ran.

Reorder export columns

  • Columns in an Export Layout can now be dragged into any order, and Excel and CSV exports follow it.
  • PDF keeps its designed fixed layout - the Custom Export window says so when PDF is selected.

Reversed clips in EDLs

  • Clips playing in reverse report their real source duration, with reverse motion recognised even when the EDL flavour leaves out the speed line, as some Avid exports do.
  • Duplicate detection and Source Pull follow reversed ranges too, so pulls cover the media actually used.
  • The Speed column shows a consistent percentage whether the sequence came in as an EDL or an XML.

Fixes and polish

  • Custom Export defaults to the Full report unless you have narrowed the view.
  • An OTIO or FCPXML file hiding behind the wrong extension is spotted and read correctly - wrong-extension handling now covers every supported format.
  • Plus small fixes and refinements throughout.
